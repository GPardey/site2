  1. Agriculture accounts for 70% of land in the UK, this has caused many bird and insect species to lose natural woodland habitats
  2. Around 42% of nearly 700 bird, mammal, butterfly and moth species have dropped in numbers since 1970
  3. One in 7 of the species mentioned above are at risk of going extinct
  4. The National Trust has committed to creating 61,776 acres of new habitats by 2025
  5. A simple nature pond in your garden can become a diverse habitat for endagered newt species and frogs
  6. By recording species you see you can greatly help the conservationists learn where and who needs the most help

    If you have a garden or frequent grassy woodland areas taking pictures greatly helps, these images will be checked by experts and be available to researchers.
